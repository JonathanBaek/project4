About Me

I was born in the Puget Sound area and have lived here all my life. I'm currently 24 and was previously studying Psychology at the University of Washington, but due to some complications, I ended up not pursuing that field. I then started working at Amazon and have been working here for around 2 years now, and because of that, I was provided the opportunity to enroll in the Correlation One program. Things I enjoy doing outside of work include watching anime/reading manga, going for walks, playing occasional video games, and trying out other new hobbies. My latest hobby that I have indulged in is custom keyboards, which is a very expensive hobby, but seeing as I don't spend too much money in other places, I feel a little less worried about buying components. I'll be sure to post photos of them as soon as I am done building them :D.


My Coding Journey

08/04/2023

Correlation One is actually the start of my coding career! Difficult to say as it's only the beginning of the road still, but I'm currently enjoying the course immensely. The teaching style and pace are right up my alley, alongside the content that it delivers. I didn't want to start this journey alone, so I invited a few coworkers/friends to join me along this journey to a both a better career path and new experience.

08/11/2023

Throughout my time so far in Correlation One, I feel as though I have learned a lot and applied those skills well into my projects. The class certainly provides enough of a challenge that I have to evaluate my options carefully, but is not impossible to the point where it feels like a insurmountable obstacle. I hope to learn more new tools I can use in my future work, as well as meet more intruiging people along the way.

08/18/2023

Things have started picking up, but currently still enjoying the course. The new challenges that arise along side things that I have learned in the past from elsewhere makes the course more engaging, especially alongside my fellow classmates. I'll have to keep on practicing more to really drill the information into my head, but I am very much looking forward to it.
